QR Codes Replacing Barcodes: The End of Unlabelled Halal Meat Racket in UK Supermarkets?

Tesco has already started replacing traditional barcodes with QR codes on fresh meat and produce. Other major chains are set to follow. Staff scan them at the till the same way. But you can now scan with your phone and access detailed supply-chain information — including whether the meat was slaughtered and blessed according to Islamic halal rules.
By Steve English | April 17, 2026 | The British Lions Roar

https://www.gs1uk.org/insights/news/Tesco-in-early-trials-of-next-generation-barcodes

This simple tech upgrade could finally expose the long-running practice: every major UK supermarket has admitted selling hundreds of millions of pounds worth of unlabelled halal meat to ordinary British shoppers who never asked for it.

All Major Supermarkets Have Admitted It

Tesco, Sainsbury’s, Marks & Spencer, Waitrose, Co-op, Morrisons and Asda have all confirmed they sell meat from halal slaughter systems — complete with the Islamic blessing invoking Allah’s name — without clear “halal” labelling on ordinary shelves.

https://www.meforum.org/islamist-watch/top-supermarkets-secretly-sell-halal-sainsburys
https://www.christian.org.uk/news/supermarkets-selling-halal-meat-without-clear-labelling/

They do it for “efficiency”: one big halal-compliant order is cheaper than running separate lines. New Zealand lamb is routinely slaughtered to halal standards for global export markets, then placed on British shelves as standard meat.

Their usual defence? “It’s pre-stunned anyway.” But the real issue is not stunning — it is the religious blessing dedicated to another faith and your right to know what you are eating.

The Numbers Don’t Lie — A Massive Surplus Sold to Non-Muslims

In 2024, an estimated 214.6 million animals were slaughtered using halal methods in England and Wales — 20.7% of all animals processed. This includes roughly 220 million chickens for the halal market.

https://questions-statements.parliament.uk/written-questions/detail/2025-03-07/36521
https://hansard.parliament.uk/commons/2025-06-09/debates/27E94862-93C7-4D29-B0DA-F77F92D14161/Non-StunSlaughterOfAnimals

Muslims and Jews combined number around 4.16 million people in England and Wales (based on the 2021 Census: 3.87 million Muslims + 0.29 million Jews).

https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/culturalidentity/religion/bulletins/religionenglandandwales/census2021
https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/culturalidentity/religion/articles/jewishidentityenglandandwales/census2021

Even assuming these communities eat double the national average (heavy meat eaters, especially chicken and lamb), their maximum consumption leaves a huge surplus:

  • Chickens: Around 128.5 million halal-slaughtered chickens (58% of the total) sold to the wider non-Muslim public.
  • Other animals (mainly sheep and cattle): Over 116 million halal-slaughtered animals beyond the direct needs of those communities.

The majority of halal-slaughtered meat and chickens in the UK therefore ends up with unwitting non-Muslims. That means the bulk of the profit from this system — including fees paid to Islamic certification bodies and charities — comes from ordinary British shoppers who were never told.

These certification organisations charge for inspections and approvals. Some operate as charities whose stated aims include promoting understanding of halal and Islamic dietary rules. The costs are passed on in the price of your meat. You pay — knowingly or not — to sustain the system.

Religious Rights for One Group, Silence for Everyone Else

UK law protects halal slaughter as a religious rite for Muslims. There is no legal requirement to label meat from animals slaughtered in accordance with religious rites unless it is specifically marketed as halal.

https://petition.parliament.uk/petitions/740004
https://www.gov.uk/guidance/halal-and-kosher-slaughter

This creates a one-way street: religious rights for one group are protected. The rights of everyone else — Christians, atheists, Jews, Hindus, Sikhs and ordinary non-Muslims — to know whether their food has been blessed in a ceremony of another faith are ignored.

When challenged, supermarkets and politicians quickly pivot to animal welfare. That misses the central point: this is about basic transparency and equal consumer rights.

QR Codes Could Finally Kill the Secrecy

Old barcodes carried almost no information. New GS1-powered QR codes link to rich digital data pages where supermarkets could easily add the slaughter method, any religious blessing, abattoir details and certification status.

One quick scan in the aisle and the truth would be there for anyone to see.

https://www.gs1uk.org/insights/news/what-two-years-of-QR-code-pilots-taught-Tesco-and-GS1-UK

The tool is here. The old system of quiet, profitable unlabelled sales — subsidised by unwitting customers and feeding millions into certification bodies — now faces real exposure.

Shoppers: start scanning. Demand the facts. No more hiding behind “efficiency” or vague wording.

The undercover halal meat trade that has fed hundreds of millions of pounds through the system without consent may finally be coming to an end.

Key facts at a glance
  • 214.6 million animals halal-slaughtered in 2024 (England & Wales)
  • Only ~4.16 million Muslims + Jews
  • Massive surplus sold unlabelled to the rest of us
  • Tesco already rolling out QR codes on meat

The barcode is dying. The excuses don’t have to.
