Green Party Cloud Cuckoo Land

What the manifesto says — and what the full policy direction reveals

By Special Correspondent · April 2026

Part I — What the Manifesto Actually Says

The Green Party manifesto commits to decriminalising the possession of all drugs.

In practice, this means removing criminal penalties for the possession of all substances — including Class A drugs such as heroin and crack cocaine — and shifting drug use out of the criminal justice system entirely.

Part II — What the Full Manifesto Adds

“A National Commission… will allow the UK to move towards a legally regulated market…”

The full manifesto goes further, setting out a clear direction toward a legally regulated drugs market.

This moves beyond decriminalisation into a long-term shift toward regulated supply.

Part III — The Policy Direction

Taken together, the policy framework points toward:

  • Removal of criminal penalties for possession
  • Shift from policing to public health model
  • Long-term move toward regulated drug supply

Part IV — The Debate

Supporters argue this represents a harm-reduction approach focused on health and safety.

Critics argue it represents a fundamental shift away from enforcement that could carry wider social consequences.

The disagreement is not about wording — but about the scale and impact of the change proposed.
